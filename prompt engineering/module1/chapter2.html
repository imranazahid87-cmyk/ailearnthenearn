Chapter 2

Understanding Large Language Models

⏱ Reading Time: 9 Minutes 📈 Difficulty: Beginner

Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) are the technology behind modern AI assistants such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot. These models are designed to understand human language and generate meaningful responses based on the instructions they receive.

As a Prompt Engineer, you do not communicate directly with Artificial Intelligence itself—you communicate with Large Language Models. Understanding how these models work will help you write better prompts and receive more accurate responses.

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand what a Large Language Model (LLM) is.
  • Learn how LLMs generate responses.
  • Identify the most popular LLMs available today.
  • Understand why LLMs are essential for Prompt Engineering.

⭐ Important Concept

An LLM does not search the internet every time you ask a question. It generates responses by recognizing language patterns learned during training. Some AI tools can also combine this with live web information when that feature is available.

🧠 How Do Large Language Models Work?

Large Language Models are trained on massive collections of text, including books, articles, websites, research papers, and publicly available documents. During training, the model learns how words, sentences, and ideas are connected.

When you enter a prompt, the model analyzes your instructions and predicts the most appropriate sequence of words to generate a useful response. It does not memorize every answer. Instead, it uses patterns learned during training to create a response that best matches your request.

The quality of the response depends on how clearly your prompt explains the task. Detailed prompts usually produce more accurate, organized, and relevant results than short or vague prompts.

🔤 Understanding Tokens

Before an AI model processes your prompt, it breaks the text into smaller pieces called tokens. A token can be a whole word, part of a word, a number, or even punctuation. The model reads these tokens instead of reading complete sentences like humans do.

For example, the sentence:

Prompt Engineering makes AI more useful.

is divided into multiple tokens before the model begins generating a response. This process allows the model to understand the relationship between different parts of your prompt.

🤖 Popular Large Language Models

💡 Real-World Example

Imagine two people asking an AI assistant to write a business email.

Person A: "Write an email."

Person B: "Write a professional email to a client thanking them for completing a project. Keep it under 200 words, use a polite tone, and end with a call to action."

The second prompt produces a much better result because it provides clear instructions, context, and formatting requirements.

🚀 Pro Tip

Large Language Models are excellent at following clear instructions. Before submitting any prompt, ask yourself: "Have I clearly explained the role, task, context, tone, and desired output?" A few extra details can dramatically improve the quality of the response.
