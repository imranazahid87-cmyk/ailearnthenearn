What is Artificial Intelligence?

⏱ Duration: 10 Minutes

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand the meaning of Artificial Intelligence.
  • Learn how AI is different from traditional software.
  • Discover where AI is used in everyday life.
  • Build a strong foundation for Prompt Engineering.

📖 What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that enables machines to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. Instead of following only fixed instructions, AI systems can analyze information, recognize patterns, understand language, solve problems, and even generate new content.

Think of AI as a digital assistant that learns from large amounts of data. The more relevant data and better instructions it receives, the more useful and accurate its responses become.

💡 Real-World Example

When you ask ChatGPT:

"Write a professional email to a client."

The AI understands your request, predicts the most appropriate response, and generates a complete email within seconds.

This is a practical example of Generative AI in action.

🚀 AI Tip

AI is powerful, but it is not magic. The quality of its output depends heavily on the quality of your prompt. Learning to communicate clearly with AI is the foundation of Prompt Engineering.

📝 Practice Activity

Open ChatGPT or another AI assistant and ask:

"What are five real-world uses of Artificial Intelligence in education?"

Observe how the AI structures its answer and think about how you could improve the prompt.

❓ Quick Quiz

  1. What does AI stand for?
  2. Can AI understand natural language?
  3. Name two everyday applications of AI.
  4. Why is AI important for Prompt Engineering?
  5. What affects the quality of AI responses?

📌 Lesson Summary

In this lesson, you learned what Artificial Intelligence is, how it differs from traditional software, where it is used, and why understanding AI is the first step toward becoming a skilled Prompt Engineer.

Understanding Large Language Models (LLMs)

⏱ Duration: 12 Minutes

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand what a Large Language Model (LLM) is.
  • Learn how ChatGPT, Gemini and Claude generate responses.
  • Know the difference between AI and LLMs.
  • Understand why LLMs are important for Prompt Engineering.

📖 What is a Large Language Model?

A Large Language Model (LLM) is an Artificial Intelligence system trained on billions of words from books, articles, websites and other public sources. Instead of memorizing answers, it learns language patterns and predicts the most appropriate next words based on your prompt.

This ability allows LLMs to answer questions, write articles, summarize documents, generate code, translate languages and assist with many everyday tasks.

⚖ AI vs Large Language Models

Artificial Intelligence Large Language Model
Broad field of computer science. A specific type of AI focused on language.
Includes robots, vision, speech and more. Specializes in understanding and generating text.
Many different technologies. One technology inside AI.

🤖 Popular LLMs

  • ChatGPT – Great for writing, coding and productivity.
  • Gemini – Strong integration with Google services and multimodal tasks.
  • Claude – Excellent for long documents and detailed reasoning.

💬 Prompt Example

Explain Cloud Computing in simple English for a beginner using real-life examples.

A good prompt clearly defines the topic, audience and expected style. This helps the model generate a better response.

💡 Pro Tip

LLMs do not "think" like humans. They generate responses by identifying patterns in language. The clearer your prompt, the more accurate and useful the output will be.

📝 Practice Exercise

Ask an AI assistant the following prompt:

Explain the Internet to a 10-year-old child using a simple real-world example.

Now rewrite the prompt by adding more details, such as tone, length or format, and compare both responses.
